Flow Dynamics of Non-Newtonian Circulating Tumor Cell (CTC) during the Passing Process through a Microfilter

Circulating Tumor cells (CTCs) are responsible for commencing cancer metastases by extravasating. Detection of CTCs can play an important role for cancer prognosis and proper treatment plans. Many techniques are used to isolate CTC from blood which is very challenging due to the extreme rarity of CTC in blood. Among all techniques, deformation-based CTC filtration poses simplicity, minimum expense and best result. For fabricating highly efficient and high throughput CTC filtration microchannel, more researches on CTC deformation through microchannel are required for extensive understanding of cell behavior. Numerical simulation offers us an easy way to interpret the cell behavior throughout deformation. In the current study, non-Newtonian cell model is adopted for CTC and the pressure signatures of CTC through a conical microchannel with different flow rates are scrutinized. The effects of different contact angles are investigated with constant flow rates. Comparisons between Newtonian and non-Newtonian models are made for analyzing the effect of shear thinning on pressure signatures and cell deformations in each case. These findings will provide significant insights into non-Newtonian cell deformation through a conical microchannel and consequential guidance for optimizing highly efficient and high throughput deformation-based CTC filtering microchip.
